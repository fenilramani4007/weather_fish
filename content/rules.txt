Fundamentals and Learning Objectives
Fundamental Principle of AI Use
The use of AI tools (such as ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, Gemini, HAWKI, etc.) is generally permitted in this course as a means of supporting your learning process. However, specific rules apply regarding permitted and prohibited use, disclosure, and academic integrity.

Explanation:
AI is part of everyday and professional life. Rather than banning it, a transparent and reflective use is encouraged. This general permission does not exempt you from the obligation to disclose AI usage and follow the principles of good academic practice. Your own work and critical thinking must remain central.

Learning Objectives Related to AI
In this course, you should develop or strengthen the following basic AI-related competencies:

Critically evaluating AI-generated content for accuracy, relevance, and potential bias.
Effectively formulating prompts to achieve precise and helpful results.
Reflectively using AI tools as a complement to your own thinking and to support learning processes.
Transparently and accurately documenting AI usage in academic contexts according to the given guidelines.
Permitted and Prohibited Use
Permitted Use Without Disclosure Requirement
The following types of AI use are allowed as personal learning support and do not require separate disclosure:

Preparing for exams individually (e.g., creating practice questions, summarizing personal notes, flashcards).
Generating explanations of concepts or technical terms for personal understanding.
Translating foreign-language texts (e.g., academic articles) for personal comprehension.
Use during explicitly designated practice phases in class (when directed by the instructor).
Spell and grammar checking using standard software (e.g., in Word).
Permitted Use With Disclosure Requirement
The following types of AI use are allowed, provided they are transparently and traceably disclosed according to the guidelines of this policy:

Support in idea generation, structuring, and outlining written assignments, homework, or presentations.
Assistance in linguistic refinement and improvement of one's own texts (beyond basic spell/grammar checking, e.g., style and wording suggestions).
Generating examples, analogies, or alternative explanations to illustrate personal arguments.
Brainstorming arguments, counterarguments, or perspectives for an analysis.
Assistance in literature research (e.g., search term suggestions, abstract summarization, source identification using "Deep Research" functions).
Code snippet generation or debugging help (if relevant to the subject).
Important:
The core performance (analysis, argumentation, critical assessment, and conclusions) must be your own work.

Prohibited AI Use
The following uses of AI are strictly prohibited in this course:

Submitting AI-generated content as your own work without proper disclosure – this may be considered academic misconduct.
Using AI during supervised exams (tests, etc.), unless explicitly permitted by exam regulations or the instructor – this may be considered academic misconduct.
Using AI to bypass learning objectives that require independent critical thinking, creative problem-solving, analytical skills, or specific methodological competencies.
Uploading copyrighted course materials or personal data to cloud-based AI systems without explicit permission (see Section 3).
Copyright and Data Protection
Use of Copyrighted Materials
The course materials provided (slides, scripts, exercises, etc.) are protected by copyright. You are not allowed to upload or enter these materials or significant parts thereof into external cloud-based AI services (such as ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, Gemini, HAWKI, etc.) for analysis or processing.

Data Protection Considerations
When using AI tools, please respect data protection principles:

Do not enter personal data (e.g., names, student IDs, email addresses, discussion posts) into external AI tools (yours or others' data, or instructors’).
Be aware that many cloud-based AI services may store your inputs and potentially use them to train their models.
Use privacy-friendly alternatives such as the university’s own AI tool HAWKI or local AI models on your own device whenever possible.
Disclosing AI Use
Format for Disclosure
Disclose your use of AI systems in written submissions as follows:

At the end of your paper (before the bibliography), include a separate section titled "Declaration of AI Tool Usage". There, list the following in bullet points:

Which AI tools you used (e.g., “ChatGPT-4, HAWKI, DeepL Write”).
For which steps in your work you used them (e.g., “Idea generation,” “Editing of introduction,” “Search terms for literature review,” “Code generation for function X”).
Example:

Declaration of AI Tool Usage
Tools used: ChatGPT-4, DeepL Write
Purpose: Brainstorming outline points, language editing of methodology section.

You may also need to submit a statement of independent work according to your examination regulations.

Practical Tips for Using AI
Prompt Strategies
To achieve better results when using AI, consider the following strategies when writing prompts:

Be specific: The more precise your question, the better the answer.
Provide context: Briefly explain the background or goal of your request.
Assign a role: Ask the AI to take on a specific role (e.g., “Explain like I’m a beginner,” “Respond as a critical reviewer”).
Specify format with examples: Tell the AI how you want the answer formatted (e.g., “List as bullet points,” “Create a table,” “Summarize in three sentences,” “Answer like in this example”).
Work iteratively: Ask follow-up questions or request revisions of the response.
Stay neutral: Avoid suggestive questions that bias the response (instead of “Is it true that X is good?”, ask “What are the pros and cons of X?”).
Example of a specific prompt:

"Explain the concept of 'Distributed Cognition' for a Master's student in education. First give a brief definition (max. 50 words), then two examples from educational contexts, and finally list three criticisms of the concept. Structure the response with clear headings. Include relevant literature sources."

Explanation:
The way you “talk” to an AI (prompting) significantly influences the quality of its response. With precise and thoughtful prompts, you’ll get more useful and relevant results for your studies.

Dealing with AI Errors (“Hallucinations” etc.)
AI systems make mistakes. Don’t rely blindly on AI-generated information. Pay special attention to:

Factual inaccuracies ("hallucinations")
Invented sources
Outdated knowledge
Incomplete or one-sided representations
Bias from training data
Verification strategies:

Check key claims and facts against academic sources.
Verify citations and references – do they exist? Are they correctly cited?
Compare responses from different AI tools or look for counter-positions.
Be especially skeptical of highly specific or surprising claims.
Use of AI by Instructors
General Notes
Explanation:
We use AI tools to improve and streamline our teaching. This is always done in accordance with legal requirements and with the goal of providing you with the best possible learning experience.

Course Preparation and Materials
We may use AI tools to support the following tasks:

Planning the structure and content of the course.
Creating and revising teaching materials (e.g., slides, summaries, visual aids).
Generating exercises or case studies.
Optimizing task descriptions or assignment instructions.
